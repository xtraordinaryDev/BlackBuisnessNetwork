Our Mission

Empower and educate Black-owned businesses in Minnesota through a supportive network led by Black leaders. Our goal is to foster economic growth, community collaboration, and entrepreneurial success while promoting land ownership within the Black community.

Our Vision

To build a thriving and sustainable ecosystem for Black-owned businesses in Minnesota by fostering economic growth, land ownership, and entrepreneurial success. Our vision is a future where Black leaders and businesses collaborate to create generational wealth, strengthen communities, and promote equitable access to opportunities.

Meet Our Team

David Frazier

President

David Frazier, President of BBN, is a community leader and advocate in Saint Paul. As the owner of Just Imagine and FlyAzz Frenchies, David is celebrated for his commitment to service and economic empowerment. Named Salvation Army Metro's Volunteer of the Year, he brings Economic Empowerment to the forefront, supporting Black-owned businesses in building resilient futures.

Kendra Lofton

Vice President

As Vice President of BBN and founder of WRLD Class Cleaning LLC and Kendra's Clubhouse, Kendra Lofton is committed to Community Engagement. Her dedication to mentoring Black entrepreneurs has made her a valued advocate, uplifting others through guidance and inspiration in Saint Paul’s business community.

Seanne Thomas

Secretary

Seanne holds a Bachelor’s in Economics from the University of Minnesota and specializes in first-time homebuyer programs, FHA financing, and down payment assistance. At ABC Realty, she works to build vibrant communities by helping families find homes. Through BBN, she promotes Equity and Inclusivity, ensuring that everyone has a chance to thrive.

Lynette Harris

Treasurer

Lynette, BBN's Treasurer, brings her passion for community-based initiatives and sustainable practices to her role. With experience from Moms Food Shelf and as a District 5 board member, Lynette promotes Innovation and Resourcefulness, supporting financial operations that drive growth and sustainability for Black-owned businesses.

Coa Murrell

Director of Fundraising and Finance

Coa Murrell, owner of Care4All, holds a Bachelor’s in Healthcare Administration. She oversees fundraising and finance for BBN, empowering entrepreneurs with strategic planning and resource management, embodying Economic Empowerment. Coa is dedicated to helping Black-owned businesses thrive.

Darius Dawson

Director of Marketing and Public Relations

Darius, a seasoned entrepreneur with expertise in real estate and business, brings Innovation and Resourcefulness to BBN. His focus on uplifting Black-owned businesses drives BBN’s marketing efforts, creating new growth opportunities for the community.

Terrell Smith

Director of Member Engagement and Programs

Terrell Smith, owner of Gentleman Cuts for over 30 years and featured on Small Business Revolution on Hulu, brings Community Engagement to BBN. Known for his community service and partnerships with ESNDC, Terrell empowers Black-owned businesses and fosters meaningful connections across Saint Paul’s Black business network.

Strategic Initiatives

Expanded Workshops

Empowering Black-owned businesses through hands-on workshops focused on leadership, financial growth, and tech integration.

BBN’s workshops provide essential training and resources for Black-owned businesses, with a focus on sustainable growth, effective leadership, and modern technology integration. These sessions equip entrepreneurs with skills in financial management, digital marketing, and leadership development, helping businesses thrive in an evolving marketplace.

Mentorship and Networking

Building a connected community of Black entrepreneurs with access to mentors and networking events that inspire growth.

Through enhanced mentorship programs and networking events, BBN fosters meaningful connections among Black business owners and professionals. Members have access to experienced mentors and industry experts who offer guidance and support, while networking events encourage collaboration and knowledge-sharing, building a strong community of Black entrepreneurs.

Community Engagement Programs

Strengthening community ties with local initiatives, volunteer events, and collaborative projects that foster unity and growth.

BBN is dedicated to strengthening community ties by focusing on initiatives that benefit both businesses and their communities. Through local events, volunteer opportunities, and community-building projects, BBN encourages members to engage actively in creating positive social impact, fostering a sense of unity, and working toward collective success.

Focus on Black-Owned Businesses

Championing Black-owned businesses through tailored resources, funding support, and advocacy for sustained success.

BBN’s commitment to supporting Black-owned businesses is central to its mission. Tailored programs address the unique challenges faced by Black entrepreneurs, providing resources and advocacy to ensure these businesses have opportunities for success. From funding options to exclusive training, BBN empowers Black-owned businesses to create lasting legacies within their communities.

Current Partners

Rooted Philanthropy

Rooted Philanthropy has been instrumental in helping BBN make a significant impact on the Black business community. Through their funding and strategic support, BBN has been able to launch initiatives that bring valuable resources, education, and connections to Black-owned businesses across Minnesota. Rooted Philanthropy’s partnership has been a stepping stone, allowing BBN to step into a broader, impactful role, ultimately enabling us to reach new heights in empowering Black entrepreneurs. Their support is truly the foundation upon which BBN has built its current success.

Partner Benefits

Visibility and Branding Opportunities

  • 1. Website Recognition: Each partner receives a dedicated spot on BBN’s Sponsor/Partner Logos section on the homepage, ensuring high visibility to site visitors.
  • 2. Social Media Spotlights: Partners are regularly highlighted on BBN’s social media channels, showcasing their commitment to community impact.
  • 3. Event Presence: Partner logos are included in all event materials, banners, and programs, providing exposure to event attendees and BBN members.

Collaborative Opportunities

  • 1. Co-Hosting Events: Partners can co-host specific events or workshops, positioning their brand as an advocate for Black-owned business empowerment.
  • 2. Panel Participation: Partner representatives may participate in BBN panels or networking events, offering expertise and connecting with BBN members.
  • 3. Exclusive Networking: BBN partners are invited to exclusive networking events where they can connect with other local leaders, business owners, and influencers within the community.

Impact and Community Engagement

  • 1. Direct Involvement in Initiatives: Partners can choose to align their support with specific initiatives (e.g., mentorship programs, workshops), allowing for targeted impact.
  • 2. Monthly Impact Updates: BBN provides quarterly reports for partners, detailing how their support has directly benefited the community, including success stories, metrics, and the overall impact of their contributions.
  • 3. Brand as a Community Leader: By partnering with BBN, organizations demonstrate their commitment to supporting Black entrepreneurs and fostering equitable economic development in Minnesota.
