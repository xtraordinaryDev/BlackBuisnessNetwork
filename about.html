Our Mission

Empower and educate Black-owned businesses in Minnesota through a supportive network led by Black leaders. Our goal is to foster economic growth, community collaboration, and entrepreneurial success while promoting land ownership within the Black community.

Our Vision

To build a thriving and sustainable ecosystem for Black-owned businesses in Minnesota by fostering economic growth, land ownership, and entrepreneurial success. Our vision is a future where Black leaders and businesses collaborate to create generational wealth, strengthen communities, and promote equitable access to opportunities.

Meet Our Team

David Frazier

President

David Frazier, President of the Black Business Network, is a respected leader and community advocate in Saint Paul. A dedicated father and the owner of Just Imagine, a clothing boutique with two locations, and FlyAzz Frenchies, David is celebrated for his commitment to service and business growth. Named Volunteer of the Year by Salvation Army Metro, he is passionate about fostering a supportive and thriving environment for Black-owned businesses across the community.

Kendra Lofton

Vice President

Kendra Lofton is the Vice President of the Black Business Network and a dedicated leader with a deep passion for service and community. As the founder of WRLD Class Cleaning LLC and Kendra's Clubhouse, and a caring mother, she works tirelessly to empower Black-owned businesses in Saint Paul. Kendra’s heart for mentorship and growth has made her an invaluable advocate for progress and sustainability within the community.

Seanne Thomas

Secretary

Seanne holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Economics from the University of Minnesota. She’s an expert in first-time homebuyer programs, FHA financing, and down payment and closing-cost assistance. Seanne approaches her work at ABC Realty from a community-building perspective, striving to help families become part of vibrant neighborhoods.

Lynette Harris

Treasurer

Lynette Harris, an urban gardening advocate from 2012 to 2015, has dedicated her career to promoting community-based initiatives and sustainable practices. She brings this passion to her role as Treasurer of the Black Business Network (BBN), where she manages the organization's financial operations. As the director of Moms Food Shelf since 2008, Lynette has gained deep insights into food insecurity and has been a strong advocate for families in need. Her role as a District 5 board member since 2017 has further developed her expertise in youth development and community advocacy.

Coa Murrell

Director of Fundraising and Finance

Coa Murrell holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Healthcare Administration from Minnesota State University and serves as the Director of Fundraising and Finance for the Black Business Network (BBN). In her role at Care4All, she oversees financial operations and healthcare services, demonstrating strong leadership in both financial and healthcare management. Coa has extensive experience supporting and mentoring small businesses, empowering entrepreneurs through strategic planning and resource management. Additionally, her work as an instructor at Care4All Lab Services has allowed her to engage youth in practical training and mentorship. Coa’s dedication to Black-owned businesses makes her a key asset to BBN's mission.

Darius Dawson

Director of Marketing and Public Relations

Darius Dawson, our Director of Marketing and Public Relations, is a seasoned entrepreneur with an impressive portfolio spanning various businesses and real estate holdings. His deep expertise and dedication to uplifting Black-owned businesses enable him to bring fresh strategies and inspiring leadership to the Black Business Network, driving growth and creating impactful opportunities for the community.

Terrell Smith

Director of Member Engagement and Programs

Terrell- was born in Chicago, southside with no intention of becoming a barber. Moved to Minnesota in 1992 where he worked as a forklift operator in a shipping and receiving department. Terrell took a chance and stepped outside his comfort zone to become a telemarketing for Jackson Hewitt. This led Terrell to go to Mn School of Barbering on Lake Street. He has been cutting hair for 30 years now and loves to give back to his community by helping the disadvantaged and homeless in his neighborhood with free cuts. This led him to a partnership with ESDNC to become part of the Small Business Revolution tv show on Amazon Prime and Hulu. Terrell is a community leader and tries to uplift the neighborhood at every corner. Terrell has been at his currant location called Gentleman Cuts for eight years. Terrell gets a sense of gratification to help someone with personal hygiene. Terrel also carries an instructor’s license to teach people the art of barbing. Terrell is currently the Director of Member Engagement and Programs for the Black Business Network St Paul.

Ky'Mari Love

Ky'Mari Love- a Business Administration graduate with a concentration in Accounting, works as an Economic Development Specialist at the East Side Neighborhood Development Company. Strategic Initiatives

Expanded Workshops

Empowering Black-owned businesses through hands-on workshops focused on leadership, financial growth, and tech integration.

BBN’s workshops provide essential training and resources for Black-owned businesses, with a focus on sustainable growth, effective leadership, and modern technology integration. These sessions equip entrepreneurs with skills in financial management, digital marketing, and leadership development, helping businesses thrive in an evolving marketplace.

Mentorship and Networking

Building a connected community of Black entrepreneurs with access to mentors and networking events that inspire growth.

Through enhanced mentorship programs and networking events, BBN fosters meaningful connections among Black business owners and professionals. Members have access to experienced mentors and industry experts who offer guidance and support, while networking events encourage collaboration and knowledge-sharing, building a strong community of Black entrepreneurs.

Community Engagement Programs

Strengthening community ties with local initiatives, volunteer events, and collaborative projects that foster unity and growth.

BBN is dedicated to strengthening community ties by focusing on initiatives that benefit both businesses and their communities. Through local events, volunteer opportunities, and community-building projects, BBN encourages members to engage actively in creating positive social impact, fostering a sense of unity, and working toward collective success.

Focus on Black-Owned Businesses

Championing Black-owned businesses through tailored resources, funding support, and advocacy for sustained success.

BBN’s commitment to supporting Black-owned businesses is central to its mission. Tailored programs address the unique challenges faced by Black entrepreneurs, providing resources and advocacy to ensure these businesses have opportunities for success. From funding options to exclusive training, BBN empowers Black-owned businesses to create lasting legacies within their communities.

Partner Benefits

Visibility and Branding Opportunities

  • 1. Website Recognition: Each partner receives a dedicated spot on BBN’s Sponsor/Partner Logos section on the homepage, ensuring high visibility to site visitors.
  • 2. Social Media Spotlights: Partners are regularly highlighted on BBN’s social media channels, showcasing their commitment to community impact.
  • 3. Event Presence: Partner logos are included in all event materials, banners, and programs, providing exposure to event attendees and BBN members.

Collaborative Opportunities

  • 1. Co-Hosting Events: Partners can co-host specific events or workshops, positioning their brand as an advocate for Black-owned business empowerment.
  • 2. Panel Participation: Partner representatives may participate in BBN panels or networking events, offering expertise and connecting with BBN members.
  • 3. Exclusive Networking: BBN partners are invited to exclusive networking events where they can connect with other local leaders, business owners, and influencers within the community.

Impact and Community Engagement

  • 1. Direct Involvement in Initiatives: Partners can choose to align their support with specific initiatives (e.g., mentorship programs, workshops), allowing for targeted impact.
  • 2. Monthly Impact Updates: BBN provides quarterly reports for partners, detailing how their support has directly benefited the community, including success stories, metrics, and the overall impact of their contributions.
  • 3. Brand as a Community Leader: By partnering with BBN, organizations demonstrate their commitment to supporting Black entrepreneurs and fostering equitable economic development in Minnesota.
